## PAGE 2 · The Reality on the Floor

**Title:** A problem we all recognise

**On screen:**
- Between 5 and 7 pm, traffic peaks while conversion hits its lowest point. The right person is in the wrong zone.
- When an experienced colleague leaves, their methods walk out the door with them.
- Development booklets get filled in, filed away, and never opened again.
- A new starter's effort is invisible to every system during their first months.

**Speaker:**
None of this is hypothetical. The hourly data from the Bornova store shows the evening pocket clearly: in the very hour the most visitors walk in, the rate at which visits turn into sales is at its lowest. Meanwhile we hold three separate training booklets, Orquest schedules, and the priceless observations that live only in our coaches' heads. The problem is not a lack of data. The problem is that none of these pieces talk to each other.

## PAGE 3 · What Pusula Is

**Title:** The coach's daily desk

**On screen:**
- Nine sections under one roof: today's queue, team, profile, development booklet, learning memory, mentor path, placement, floor plan, impact.
- It does not replace existing tools. It sits on top of Orquest, One Store and the booklets as a layer of intelligence.
- The founding principle is zero extra workload: the coach keeps doing the job they already do, and the system learns from it.

**Speaker:**
Pusula is not a reporting screen. It is the desk a coach opens in the morning and closes at night. The day starts on a single page: pending approvals, discovery opportunities, mentor pairings and open booklet topics, all in one queue. Nothing asks you to switch to another program. Approving and planning happen right where you are, in one touch.

## PAGE 4 · People First Is Not a Slogan, It Is an Architectural Decision

**Title:** No scores, no rankings, the coach decides

**On screen:**
- Nobody gets a score and nobody is ranked against anyone.
- Evidence is attached to the suggestion, never to the person.
- An untried area is not a weakness. The system reads it as no data yet and proposes a discovery shift.
- Employees can see their own profile. The purpose is development, not selection.
- Everything the system produces is a suggestion. The final word always belongs to the coach.

**Speaker:**
This page is the heart of the presentation. Most comparable systems score people, rank them, and burn through the floor's trust within a week. We built the opposite into the architecture itself. Open the team screen and you will see thirty people, but you will not find a single score. If Asya has never worked the fitting rooms, her profile does not say weak. It says untried, and the system suggests a discovery shift in a quiet hour, alongside an experienced colleague. This approach keeps us on solid ground ethically and legally at the same time.

## PAGE 5 · The Evidence Engine: Where a Suggestion Comes From

**Title:** Every suggestion can be held to account

**On screen:**
- Three evidence channels: person-linked measurements such as the fitting room counter, shift overlap analysis, and booklet marks combined with coach observations.
- Every suggestion card opens a why this suggestion view: the triggering signal, the evidence channel, the inference step and the confidence level, in order.
- The coach applies, edits, or rejects with a reason. Even a rejection trains the system; the reason is written back into the model.

**Speaker:**
Trust in AI starts with it not being a black box. In Pusula, no suggestion falls from the sky. Open a card on the period action screen, ask why this suggestion, and you see a four-step chain: which signal triggered it, which channel the evidence came from, which method worked in similar profiles, and how confident the system actually is. When it is not confident, it says so. We do not hide that early suggestions are generic while the system is cold. That honesty is not a weakness of the product; it is the precondition for people adopting it.

## PAGE 6 · The Company's Memory Book

**Title:** No mastery is ever lost

**On screen:**
- Development Booklet: the 120-topic booklet goes digital. Every mark keeps its date, every status keeps its own note, permanently.
- Learning Memory: the coach writes an observation, the system recognises the scenario and the method, and the approved knowledge is filed into the archive.
- Repeated observations become patterns, and patterns become curriculum proposals. The training plan turns into a living document.
- Mentor Path: even if the master leaves, their method is already encoded in company memory. The next coach picks up where things left off, not from zero.

**Speaker:**
The most expensive loss in retail is not revenue, it is knowledge. No handbook describes how a ten-year veteran manages the fitting room queue at peak hour. The day she leaves, that knowledge leaves with her. In Pusula the coach writes a two-line observation, the system tags it with a scenario and a method, the coach confirms, and from that moment the method belongs to the company. When the same theme repeats across three different people, the system raises its hand and proposes moving that module earlier in the training plan. The curriculum stops being a form updated once a year and becomes something the shop floor feeds every week.

## PAGE 7 · On the Floor: The Right Hands, the Right Place, the Right Hour

**Title:** The evening pocket closes on evidence

**On screen:**
- Placement: the traffic forecast meets each person's evidence. The coach approves suggestions one by one and watches the line-up take shape.
- The floor plan knows the store: where the pressure is, where the proven hands are, and where the gap sits.
- Idle colleagues are routed to support work automatically. Productivity improves by method, not by guesswork.

**Speaker:**
All of this development infrastructure becomes very concrete at five in the evening. The system looks at tomorrow's traffic, knows who has proven evidence in which area, and proposes a placement for the peak. The coach sees every move on screen and accepts or changes each one individually. Lay the pressure layer over the evidence layer on the floor plan and the gap reveals itself. That gap is exactly where the placement suggestion is born.

## PAGE 8 · Impact and Vision

**Title:** It starts cold and sharpens with every closed loop

**On screen:**
Pilot targets (representative, to be validated on the floor):
- Suggestion accuracy from 62 to 86 percent over four periods.
- New starter ramp-up shortened by two weeks.
- The coach's plan and report preparation load drops visibly; drafts arrive ready.
- Master knowledge retained in the company, in full.

Vision:
- From the Bornova pilot to the store network: every store builds its own memory, and the network learns from a shared curriculum.
- The goal is not a technology showcase. It is a way of working that shortens the coach's day, makes the employee's effort visible, and protects the company's collective mind.

**Speaker:**
Let me be direct about the numbers: these are pilot targets, and they will be validated by backtesting as real floor data arrives. The strongest part of our claim is this: the system learns from every loop it closes. As coaches approve and real outcomes are written back, suggestions become personal and accuracy climbs. The screen sitting on one coach's desk in Bornova today can become part of a company memory speaking the same language in every store tomorrow. Pusula starts with people and manages with evidence. I would be glad to take your questions.
